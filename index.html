01 — The Journey

From raw data to a reasoning system

Every step below was a concept learned in the course and then applied for real on the IBM HR dataset.

  1. Understanding the data

    Loaded 1,470 employee records, dropped non-informative columns, handled the target, and confirmed a clean dataset with a meaningful class imbalance (~16% leavers) — the single fact that shaped every decision afterwards.

  2. Exploratory Data Analysis

    Twelve visual stories — overtime, income, age, job role, marital status, satisfaction, tenure — to find the real drivers of attrition before modelling anything.

  3. Preprocessing — scaling & normalizing

    Learned why feature scaling matters: standardizing features let Logistic Regression converge in 18 iterations instead of failing after 1,000, and made its coefficients interpretable. Compared standardization vs min-max vs row-normalization and chose the right tool.

  4. Modelling — from one tree to ensembles

    Logistic Regression → Decision Tree → Random Forest, then the ensemble family: Bagging (parallel, variance↓), Boosting (sequential, bias↓), and Stacking (a learned blend of all three).

  5. Evaluation — beyond accuracy

    Confusion matrices, precision/recall, F1, ROC-AUC, and cross-validation. Learned that on imbalanced data accuracy lies — and that the threshold you classify at is a design decision, not a default.

  6. Interpretation & action

    Turned coefficients into a human-readable "why", and a 13-rule engine into personalised retention recommendations. A model is only useful when someone can act on it.

02 — Flagship Project

HR Employee Attrition Predictor

A complete Python ML system on the IBM HR Analytics dataset — data in, decisions out.

What it does

Loads and preprocesses the data, trains and tunes six models, evaluates them with a full metric suite, generates every EDA and comparison chart, then lets a user enter any employee — manually or from a CSV — to predict whether they'll leave or stay and receive prioritised, personalised HR actions to retain them.

Key insight

Overtime is the strongest signal — employees who work overtime leave at 30.5% vs 10.4%. Low pay, single status, no stock options and early tenure compound the risk.

03 — What I Learned

Concepts, applied — not just studied

Each card is a course topic I implemented and reasoned about in the project.

Data Preprocessing

Cleaning, label encoding, train/test split with stratification to preserve class ratios.

Feature Scaling

Standardization for distance/gradient models; why trees don't need it. Convergence and interpretability gains measured.

Normalization

Min-max vs z-score vs L2 — and why row-normalization is wrong for tabular data.

Logistic Regression

A calibrated, interpretable linear baseline — and the project's most precise model.

Decision Trees

Pre-pruning with depth and leaf constraints to fight overfitting.

Random Forest

Bagged, feature-randomised trees for stable predictions and importance scores.

Bagging

Bootstrap aggregation — parallel trees that cut variance.

Boosting

Sequential trees that correct prior errors — the best recall, catching ~70% of leavers.

Stacking

A meta-model that blends base learners — the best ROC-AUC of the lineup.

Confusion Matrix

TN / FP / FN / TP — seeing which errors a model makes, not just how many.

Metrics & F1

Precision, recall, F1, ROC-AUC — choosing the metric that fits the business cost.

Threshold & Imbalance

Tuning the decision threshold to 0.40 to lift recall and F1 on imbalanced data.

Cross-Validation

5-fold GridSearchCV for honest, leakage-free hyperparameter tuning.

Model Interpretation

Coefficient × value contributions to explain every individual prediction.

Accuracy isn't enough

An 84% "predict everyone stays" baseline looks fine but catches zero leavers. Our model adds +4.4 real points while genuinely flagging at-risk staff.

Trade-offs are choices

Lowering the threshold trades a little accuracy for more recall — the right call when a missed leaver costs far more than a false alarm.

The best model is a blend

Stacking combined the strengths of all three base learners to top both ROC-AUC and accuracy.
